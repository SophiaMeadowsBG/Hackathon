02The problem

Cooling demand peaks at the exact hours electricity is most expensive — and dirtiest.

Homes heat up through the afternoon and people arrive home into the 4–7 pm price peak, just as solar output collapses. Every new AC unit adds load to the worst possible window. Scaled to millions of homes, that means reinforced networks, peaking plants, and bills nobody chose.

03The insight

Store cold, not electrons.

A phase-change material absorbs enormous heat while it melts — at a constant temperature. Freeze it overnight on cheap, low-carbon electricity; let it melt through the peak, absorbing the home's heat while the compressor stays off. The plateau is the battery.

Crystallisation front propagating through a PCM stack — charging (dark = frozen) and discharging (light = melted), on repeat, every night and every peak.
PCM thermal store
~£60/kWh of cooling
  • No cycle degradation — melt/freeze indefinitely
  • No inverter, no export licence, no rare metals
  • Charges at friendly evaporator temps (higher COP than ice)
  • Passive discharge: fan-only power draw
Lithium battery route
~£180/kWh of cooling*
  • Degrades with every cycle
  • Needs inverter + G99 export approval
  • Competes for the battery with every other home load
  • *£400–500/kWh installed ÷ COP ≈ 3

Bonus physics: night air is 8–12 °C cooler than the afternoon, so the compressor itself runs at a higher COP while charging — the same cooling costs fewer kWh, before the price spread is even counted.

06Technology stack

Sense → predict → decide → act.

The intelligence layer is a model-predictive controller on a rolling 24-hour horizon: minimise cost and carbon, subject to hard comfort constraints. Everything below it is commodity hardware; everything above it is software Centrica already knows how to run at fleet scale.

Sense

Instrumentation

  • Room temp / RH + occupancy (Zigbee)
  • PCM state-of-charge via probe array + enthalpy balance (temp is flat mid-melt — you must meter energy in/out)
  • CT-clamp power metering
  • Edge MCU · MQTT uplink
Predict

Forecast models

  • Day-ahead tariff API (Agile-style)
  • Weather forecast ingestion
  • Cooling-load forecast (gradient boosting)
  • Grey-box RC thermal model of the home, identified from live data
Decide

MPC optimiser

  • Rolling 24 h horizon, re-solved each interval
  • Objective: £ + gCO₂ minimised
  • Constraints: comfort band, PCM capacity, compressor limits
  • Falls back to safe thermostat logic offline
Act & aggregate

Control + fleet

  • Compressor, diverter valve, fan control
  • Smart-home ecosystem integration
  • DSR event response (OpenADR-style signals)
  • Fleet: thousands of stores = a dispatchable virtual cooling plant
07Why it matters

Small per home. Enormous per fleet.

For the household

Shift ~8–10 kWh of cooling out of the peak on a hot day. At a 20p+/kWh spread that's roughly £2 a day through a heatwave — and the compressor runs when it's most efficient, so total energy falls too, not just cost.

daily peak saving
≈ £1.50–2.20 per hot day
comfort
Unchanged — same cool air, different hours of purchase

For the grid — and the business

100,000 cassettes each avoiding ~1 kW at peak is ~100 MW of dispatchable demand reduction — a virtual cooling plant, bid into flexibility markets the way PeakSave already aggregates heating. Cooling is the next load; whoever owns its flexibility owns the customer relationship.

fleet effect
100k homes ≈ 100 MW of peak relief
stacked revenue
Tariff arbitrage + DSR payments + avoided reinforcement
